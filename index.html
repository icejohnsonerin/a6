Artist Statement

2017 Statement

​Artist Statement: Erin Ice-Johnson

I create spaces that alter people’s perception of space and time using technology. The way our brains’ perceive individual moments are what makes up our greater experience of life. In my work I am motivated by providing whole new experiences to the viewer in order to change their world, whether that be temporary or permanent. While other media can do this, I feel most compelled to communicate through isolating and enveloping people in uncanny interactive spaces in a way that engages several of the senses simultaneously. For example, in The Figure Suspended, the viewer walks down a hallway between three offset, projected, obscured figures, which with extreme slowness repeat everyday movements. The sensation of walking between the large looming figures makes the viewer uncomfortable and self-conscious. My goal is to create those sensations so strongly that the viewer forgets all else and is suspended

in that moment. I am inspired by the work of the Light and Space movement in which artists created site specific installations focusing on the perception of light, scale, and surface. The work of Robert Irwin inspires me due to its minimalistic use of space, which provides completely unique experiences. While I try to create the same sensations with my work, I also move beyond the media and method of the Light and Space movement by using contemporary technology. I've used technology such as projection, video, programing, 3D modeling and printing, laser cutting and engraving, and robotics in installations and also traditional media, such as, sculpture, drawing, and printmaking. In the piece New Age, I used 3D modeling to create a figure of a hand that I then 3D printed and then cast in bronze using the ancient luted crucible lost wax method. The resulting piece both comments on and utilizes the evolution of tools throughout human history, which is an overarching theme in my work.
